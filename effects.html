Effects of Codeine

Short-Term Effects

Drowsiness & Sleepiness

Drowsiness is one of the first things people notice when they take codeine. Your brain gets slowed down by the drug, which makes you feel super tired. You might not be able to focus on school, driving, or other activities. This happens because codeine affects parts of your brain that keep you alert.

Nausea & Vomiting

Nausea is a feeling of sickness in your stomach that can make you want to throw up. Codeine affects your stomach and a part of your brain called the chemo trigger zone, which controls vomiting. This happens pretty quickly after taking it. The feeling can last for a while and makes people feel really uncomfortable.

Dizziness & Lightheadedness

Dizziness is when the room seems to spin around you or you feel unbalanced. Codeine affects your brain and can mess with your sense of balance. Your inner ear helps you stay balanced, and codeine can confuse that system. This makes people feel like they might fall over, which can be really dangerous.

Constipation

Constipation means you can't go to the bathroom normally. Codeine slows down your whole digestive system, including your stomach and intestines. Food moves slower through your body, which means your body pulls out too much water. This makes it hard and painful to have a bowel movement.

Long-Term Effects

Addiction & Dependence

Addiction happens when your brain gets used to codeine and starts needing it to feel normal. Your body builds up a tolerance, meaning you need more and more to feel the same effect. Dependence is when your body gets so used to the drug that it's hard to function without it. Both addiction and dependence can ruin your life and make you spend lots of money.

Memory Problems

Long-term use of codeine can damage your memory and learning ability. Your brain's memory centers, like the hippocampus, can get hurt from constant drug use. You might struggle to remember things from school or forget important events. This can hurt your grades and your future.

Depression & Mental Health Issues

Using codeine for too long can mess with your emotions and cause depression. Your brain makes chemicals that control your mood, and codeine changes these chemicals. Over time, you might feel sad, hopeless, or not interested in things you used to like. Depression can make life really hard and suicidal thoughts can happen.

Increased Overdose Risk

The longer you use codeine, the higher your risk of overdosing. Your body gets used to the drug, so you take more and more. Eventually, you might take too much and your body can't handle it. An overdose can stop your breathing completely and cause death.
